Serious Eats

Serious Eats delivers quality food blogging on a photo heavy layout. It's content covers everything from restaurant reviews, to reverse-engineering Mcdonalds french fries, and fool-proof recipes for the average foodie. It uses low-contrast coloring in it's layout to help elevate the vivid food photographs. The site also recommend similar articles based on the article you've chosen. SE has made small subtle changes to it's layout over time which has help enhance the user experience by presenting content in a more organized way.


Deadspin

Deadspin offers uncensored commentary on the sports world and risque topics that appeal to the average male sports fan. With it's high turnover in writers the site holds many different views which appeals to a wider demographic. Often providing major sports news instaneously by circumventing the traditional editorial steps that major sports sites like ESPN, NBA and NFL are bound by. Visually they're constantly making changes to the layout which can frustrate the sites regulars.


Google

Google employs the KISS principle when they designed the layout of the worlds largest search engine. A big centered logo and a simple search bar over a plain white background makes up it's home page. Lean and fast made it a clear stand out from search engines who are bombarded with advertisements, images and content. It's layout see's little change aside from the custom fonts they use for holidays and special occasions. Making the valuable frontpage real-estate off-limits to advertisement, the few links you do see on the front page leads you to Googles own apps.
